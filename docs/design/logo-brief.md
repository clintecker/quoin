# Quoin — logo & app icon vibes brief

*For Claude design (or any designer) producing the mark and the macOS app
icon. The icon is currently a launch BLOCKER (launch-ledger L6).*

## The name is the concept — use it

**Quoin** (pronounced "coin") has two meanings, and both are exactly this
app:

1. **Letterpress**: a quoin is the expanding metal wedge a printer tightens
   to lock type into the chase before printing. It is the small, precise
   tool that holds everything else perfectly in place. Quoin-the-app does
   the same for markdown: it locks your text into a beautiful typeset form
   without ever deforming the type itself (byte-lossless files).
2. **Masonry**: quoins are the dressed cornerstones of a building — the
   structural, slightly-proud blocks at the edge that everything true is
   measured from. Blocks. Structure. Corners. A markdown document IS a
   stack of blocks.

Either metaphor alone can carry the mark. The letterpress wedge is the
richer, rarer story (a typography tool for a typography app); the
cornerstone is the more geometric, more block-editor story. A mark that
quietly reads as both is the jackpot.

## What the app is (for flavor, not literal depiction)

A native macOS markdown editor where the rendered page is the editor.
Instant at any document size. Diagrams and math render natively and
re-render live as you type. Plain `.md` files on disk, nothing leaves your
Mac, zero web engine. It is a craftsman's tool: quiet, fast, honest.

## Personality words

Precise · warm · unhurried · trustworthy · type-nerd · Mac-assed ·
letterpress-meets-2026. NOT: startup-y, playful-blobby, corporate-flat,
terminal-hacker, AI-sparkle.

## Visual language it must live beside

The upcoming UI (docs/design/ui-refresh-prep.md): soft cool-gray canvas
`#F2F3F5`, floating white cards radius ~14, rounded geometric sans (SF
Rounded-adjacent), vivid blue accent ≈ `systemBlue #3478F6`, muted slate
secondary `#8A96A8`, saturated pastel highlight bands (lime, lavender).
The signature dark surface in the app is the code canvas `#1E2430` — a
deep ink-blue-charcoal that would make a handsome icon background.

## Directions worth exploring (in preference order)

1. **The wedge.** A letterpress quoin — two opposed wedges, or a wedge
   with its key/screw — abstracted to 2–3 geometric shapes. Angled block
   locking against straight blocks: tension, precision, lock-in-place.
   Could double as an abstract "Q".
2. **The cornerstone Q.** A squared spiral / cornered form where dressed
   blocks turn the corner and imply a Q with its tail. Reads as
   blocks-of-a-document at small sizes.
3. **Type-high.** A single piece of movable type seen at an angle, face
   showing a "Q" — but only if it can survive 16 px.

## Hard requirements

- macOS 26-era app icon: works inside the system squircle, layered/specular
  treatment welcome but the silhouette must carry it.
- Legible at 16 px (menu bar/Finder list) through 1024 px.
- Light and dark mode variants; must sit well on both the soft-gray canvas
  and the `#1E2430` ink surface.
- One-color version must exist (document proxy icon, favicon, press).
- The mark should feel at home next to SF Symbols weights — geometric,
  optically corrected, no hairlines that vanish at small sizes.

## Avoid

Feather/quill/pen/pencil clichés. The "M↓" markdown mark (it's a format,
not our brand). Gradients-as-personality. Paper sheets with folded
corners. Anything that says "web app". Coins/currency (the pronunciation
trap — the wedge story beats the pun).

## Deliverables

1024 px master (layered), macOS .icns stack / asset-catalog set, one-color
SVG, dark-mode variant, and a one-line rationale we can quote on the
website.
